Security

Co-op City is a secure community with its own private public safety department. Outdoor areas are well lit. More than 700 cameras are installed, including one in every high-rise building elevator, laundry room, entrance and exit.

Library

The Baychester branch of The New York Public Library has been serving Co-op City and the surrounding community since it opened on March 13, 1973. The Baychester Library’s staff is dedicated to providing information, education, culture, and inspiration to the community of Co-op City.

Schools

A 25-acre educational park includes three elementary schools, two middle schools, a high school and a planetarium, among other things.

Parking

Parking is available in either well-lit parking garages or on the street. The garages are attended 24/7/365 and security video cameras are installed at the entrances, exits and in the elevators.

Shopping

There are three shopping centers on Co-op City property. The new indoor mall, The Mall at Bay Plaza, contains stores like Macy's, H&M and JCPenney's,Bob’s Discount Furniture, a Stop & Shop and a 13-screen movie theater. Residents can shop at different stores like Staples, P.C. Richard's, Marshall's, Toys-R-Us, Pathmark, Barnes and Noble, as well as a number of smaller stores.

Restaurants

Residents can find restaurants such as Red Lobster, Applebees, Outback, Olive Garden, Panera Bread and Starbucks.
